Orbital Lunar Fly-By Mission

Project Overview

Simulated and visualized spacecraft mission trajectories using NASA GMAT, focusing on orbital mechanics and trajectory design. The project explores spacecraft motion, transfer orbits, and mission sequencing to understand real-world astrodynamics and space mission planning.

Innovation & Contribution

I designed a trajoectory that satellites can use to save fuel costs, since the Moon's gravity will be used as a slingshot along the way to reach the desired place.

Engineering Thought Process

My thinking process was iterative and exploratory. I started with simple Earth-centered orbits to build intuition, then gradually introduced more complexity such as transfers and multi-body effects. Many of the early simulations did not behave as expected, which forced me to slow down and question my assumptions about reference frames, propagation settings, and maneuver timing. Over time, this trial-and-error approach helped me connect mathematical orbital mechanics with real spacecraft behavior, turning GMAT from a simulation tool into a way of thinking about space missions.
