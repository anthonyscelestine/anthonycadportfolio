Toroidal Propeller

Project Overview

This project focuses on the conceptual design and CAD development of a toroidal propeller aimed at reducing tip vortices, acoustic noise, and improving wake efficiency for UAV propulsion systems.

Innovation & Contribution

Toroidal Propellers have been a topic of discussion in many fields except military, where I personally believed toroidal propellers will truly fuflfil their purpose. I worked on integrating toroidal propellers into military UAVs for improved stealth, which would massively benefit clandestine operations

Engineering Thought Process

If there is one factor that must be considered when building a military UAV, is stealth. Any UAV with the conventional blade propellers can be easily detected because of the noise they make due to tiny disturbances of air called vortices that form in the sharp ends. What's the best way to remove these vortices? Make sure you have no sharp ends. And what's the best shape with the least sharp edges while still be aerodynamically ideal? A toroid. After experimental validation it was clear that 3 toroids are necessary to create one propeller for the most efficient performance.
